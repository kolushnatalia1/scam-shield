Scam Shield Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 6, 2026

Scam Shield analyzes links on webpages you open in your browser to estimate risk and show warning context. Link analysis is performed locally in your browser.

What Data Is Processed

  • Link URLs found on webpages where the extension runs (http:// and https:// pages).
  • Local risk assessment output (risk level, risk score, and short reasons).
  • A local history of recent suspicious links (up to 20 items).

How Data Is Used

  • To show link risk hints when you hover over links.
  • To show a warning before opening links assessed as high risk.
  • To show recent suspicious-link history in the extension popup.

Where Data Is Stored

Data is stored locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local and remains on your device.

Data Sharing and Sale

  • Scam Shield does not sell personal information.
  • Scam Shield does not share your link history with third parties.
  • Scam Shield does not transmit your suspicious-link history to external servers.

Permissions and Why They Are Required

  • storage: saves local extension settings and suspicious-link history.
  • tabs: detects the active tab and applies on/off protection state per tab.
  • Content script access to http://*/* and https://*/*: required to analyze links on pages where protection runs.

Control Over Your Data

You can clear suspicious-link history at any time in the extension popup using the "Clear history" button.

Scam Shield is designed to provide calm, transparent protection: it explains risk signals and helps you make safer browsing decisions.
